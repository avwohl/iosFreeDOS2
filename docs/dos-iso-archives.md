# DOS CD-ROM ISO Archives on Internet Archive

Direct `.iso` downloads suitable for the disk catalog. All are
shareware/freeware/public domain collections hosted on archive.org.

## Simtel MS-DOS Archives

The Simtel archive was the largest curated collection of MS-DOS freeware
and shareware, mirrored worldwide from the 1980s through the late 1990s.
Contents: utilities, programming tools, drivers, text editors, file
managers, archivers, communications software, and games.

## Walnut Creek CD-ROMs

Walnut Creek CDROM (founded 1991 by Bob Bruce) was one of the first
commercial distributors of free software on CD-ROMs, and ran ftp.cdrom.com,
one of the busiest FTP sites on the early internet.

## Notes

- All Simtel/Walnut Creek ISOs are ISO-9660 format, mountable as CD-ROM in DOSBox
- Archive.org serves raw ISOs as direct downloads (no zip extraction needed)
- FreeDOS ISOs require zip extraction before use
- CICA collections are Windows shareware, not DOS -- skip
- License: these are shareware/freeware/public domain compilations; no age rating concerns
